PEOPLE

Who we are

Principal Investigator:
Prof. Dr. Lauren Fink

I'm an asst. Professor in the Dept. of Psychology, Neuroscience & Behaviour, also affiliated with the McMaster Insitute for Music & the Mind, the School of Computational Science & Engineering, the Neuroscience Graduate Program, and the Centre for Advanced Research in Experimental and Applied Linguistics. Prior to coming to McMaster and starting the BEAT Lab, I lived in Germany, California, England, and Ohio. My training as an orchestral percussionist with a focus on "experimental music" informs many of my research interests. I'm grateful to have the opportunity to work with incredible students and collaborators at McMaster and across the globe!

PhD Student:
Shreshth Saxena

I'm interested in scalable methods of eye-tracking and their application to study audiovisual attention in dynamic real-world, music listening contexts. Prior to my Ph.D., I graduated with a Bachelors and Masters in Computer Science from University of Delhi, India, and gained valuable experience working as a Computer Vision developer in the industry. I'm always excited to discuss accessible technologies, mathematical/programming challenges, retro games and guitar riffs!

PhD Student:
Maya Flannery

I am a first year PhD student interested in understanding how and why we like music through individual listening experiences. I have completed a BA and MSc in Psychology, Neuroscience & Behaviour at McMaster University with a specialization in music cognition. Throughout my studies, I have developed a strong interest in Open Science issues and in creating an inclusive and accessible scientific community for everyone. Outside of academia, I am an amateur musician, enjoy being outdoors, and love sci-fi, fantasy and role-playing games.

Masters Student:
Joshua Schlichting

I'm a Master student in Psychology interested in the perception and production of time in music, as well as the social and lifespan psychology of music. In my research, I strive to integrate a broad and balanced range of empirical methods. Before moving to Canada, I studied Psychology and Musicology at the University of Heidelberg and visited music psychology labs in Frankfurt, Durham and Cambridge. Now I'm excited to join the vibrant community at McMaster! Besides my research, I'm passionate about pop culture, musicals, and environmental issues.
